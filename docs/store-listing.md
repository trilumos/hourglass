## App name  (max 30 chars)

**The title is the single strongest ranking factor on Google Play, and the proven pattern is
`Brand: Primary Keyword(s)`** — e.g. Forest's actual title is **"Forest: Focus for Productivity"**
(29), and Play's own guidance is "if your title is just your brand name, you're invisible for every
keyword search." A bare **"Sustain"** ranks poorly (common word + competes with sustainability/ESG
apps), so we append a keyword phrase — exactly like the competitors you noticed.

The brand stays **`Sustain`** (founder-LOCKED). The lock says don't make a *compound brand* like
`SustainFocus`/"Focus & Flow" — appending a descriptor after a colon in the **store title** (ASO
metadata, not the wordmark) is the standard move and respects that.

- **Recommended:** **`Sustain: Focus & Study Timer`** (28) — captures three high-intent searches in
  one title: *focus timer*, *study timer*, and *study/focus*. Best discoverability.
- Cleaner/leaner alt: **`Sustain: Deep Focus Timer`** (25) — *deep focus*, *focus timer*.
- Minimal alt: **`Sustain: Focus Timer`** (20).

*(Drop my earlier "Sustain — Focus Training" / "Focus & Flow" — off-pattern and off-lock. The
in-app wordmark stays plain "Sustain" regardless.)*

## ASO / keyword strategy (researched 2026)

**How Play ranks you:** the **title** carries the most weight, then the **short description**, then the
**full description** (all three are indexed). Reviews/ratings velocity and retention matter a lot too —
so the closed-test → good first reviews step is part of ASO, not separate from it.

**Keyword difficulty (don't fight the giants head-on):** "pomodoro timer" (difficulty ~78) and
"focus timer" (~66) are saturated — a brand-new app won't rank top for them quickly. "pomodoro"
alone is ~45. The win for a new app is **long-tail + less-saturated** phrases plus strong retention.

**Target keywords, tiered:**
- **Primary (in title + short desc):** focus timer, study timer, focus, study, deep work.
- **Secondary (in short + full desc):** pomodoro, flow, focus app, concentration, stay focused,
  focus training, streak, productivity.
- **Long-tail (achievable rankings — weave into full desc):** deep work timer, flow timer, calm focus
  app, focus and recovery, study focus timer, focus habit, distraction-free timer.

**Quick-ranking tactics:**
1. **Lead the title and the first line of the short/full description with keywords** (Play weights early text). Done above.
2. **Don't keyword-stuff** — Play penalizes it. Each term 1–2× max, read naturally.
3. **Reviews velocity:** seed the closed test with people who'll leave honest 5★ reviews in week one — the single biggest mover for a new app.
4. **Localize** the listing (esp. **Hindi** for the India-first market) — far less competition in non-English locales = easy early rankings + your home market.
5. **Iterate:** after launch, watch which terms convert in Play Console "Store performance" and rotate weak words out of the short description (custom store listings let you A/B test).

**Optional (high-value, founder's call):** ADHD is a huge, genuine search term for focus apps (Forest
targets it). If comfortable, add one honest line to the full description — e.g. "Many people who
struggle to focus, including those with ADHD, use a calm timer like this." Avoid any medical claim.
